## Overview

This is an R Shiny application for visualizing reach trajectory data collected in the lab. Raw movement data from motor learning experiments can be dense and high-dimensional — this tool makes it interactive and interpretable.

## Features

- **Trajectory plotting** — render individual or averaged reach paths across participants and conditions
- **Data transformations** — rotate, normalize, and align trajectories to a common reference frame
- **Time-point labeling** — annotate specific events within a movement (e.g., peak velocity, endpoint)
- **Clustering** — optional unsupervised clustering to identify distinct movement strategy subgroups across trials

The clustering approach was particularly useful for separating participants who showed explicit re-aiming from those relying on implicit sensorimotor adaptation — a key distinction in visuomotor learning research.
